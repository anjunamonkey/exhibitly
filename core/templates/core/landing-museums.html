Common Questions

We've heard the concerns

Every objection has shaped how we built Exhibitly. Here's how we think about the hard questions.

The concern

"Digital replaces the physical experience - why would people still visit?"

Digital is a teaser, not a substitute. Research consistently shows that online previews increase physical attendance - the same way film trailers sell cinema tickets. Exclusive in-person experiences, events, and the irreplaceable encounter with original works will always remain unique. Exhibitly is designed to inspire visits, not replace them.

The concern

"Making temporary exhibitions permanent removes the urgency to visit."

You choose exactly what goes online and when. During a live run, digital content can be limited to highlights and teasers only - the full archival experience unlocks afterwards, or remains behind an institutional access tier. "Currently showing" and "archived" are clearly distinguished throughout the platform, preserving that live sense of urgency.

The concern

"We're worried about losing control of our curatorial narrative."

Your curators' voices are the product. Exhibitly is built around structured curatorial commentary, not open crowd annotation. You control every label, audio guide, contextual note, and guided tour path. We provide the canvas - the authorship remains entirely yours.

The concern

"We don't want our prestige diluted by an 'entertainment' platform."

Exhibitly is not a social media platform or entertainment service. It is education-first - designed to reflect the authority, depth, and cultural weight of its partner institutions. There are no trending algorithms, no user-generated reviews competing with curatorial text. Your museum's brand identity, voice, and prestige are preserved and amplified, not flattened.

The concern

"We don't have the budget or staff to digitise our exhibitions."

We've built partnerships specifically to address this. Our digitisation support network offers photography, 3D modelling, and content management services at reduced cost for Exhibitly partners. For qualifying institutions, upfront digitisation costs can be offset against future revenue share - meaning no out-of-pocket risk to get started.

The concern

"Some of our exhibitions have content that isn't suitable for children."

Age-gating is built into the platform at the content level, not as an afterthought. Each exhibition is published with an audience tier - Kids, Teens, General - with separate tailored versions where appropriate. Adult exhibitions are only accessible via verified adult accounts. Your team defines the rules; the platform enforces them automatically.
