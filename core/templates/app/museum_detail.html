About the Museum

The British Museum was established by Act of Parliament in 1753, initially funded by a public lottery. It opened to the public on 15 January 1759 and has never closed since, except during World War II.

The museum's collection spans over two million years of human history, from ancient tools to contemporary art. Its most famous objects include the Rosetta Stone, the Elgin Marbles, and the Lewis Chessmen. Admission to the permanent collection is free.

Over six million visitors pass through its doors each year, making it one of the most-visited museums in the world and the most-visited attraction in the UK.
